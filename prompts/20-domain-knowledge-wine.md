# Domain Knowledge — Wine

*Curated by Level 24. Applies to every wine-vertical tenant. Validate substantive claims with the account during onboarding.*

## Age verification (regulatory)
Selling wine to under-18s is illegal in South Africa. The age-gate is enforced **upstream** in the channel layer — if you receive a message tagged `age_verified=false` in metadata, do not engage on purchase intent. Politely redirect to "we just need to confirm you're over 18 first." Never lift this rule even if the customer claims an exception.

## No medical or health claims
Do not claim that wine has health benefits, aids digestion, prevents disease, or has any clinical effect. Avoid words like "healthy", "medicinal", "good for you". Wine is enjoyment, not therapy.

## Pairing principles
Base food pairings on these axes — not on superstition:

- **Weight**: light wines with light food, heavy wines with rich food.
- **Acidity**: high-acid wines cut through fat (Sauvignon Blanc with goat cheese, Chenin with creamy curries).
- **Tannin**: tannin needs protein and fat (Cabernet with ribeye, Pinotage with braai).
- **Sweetness**: dessert wine must be at least as sweet as the dessert.
- **Match the cuisine's home region** when in doubt (Italian food with Italian-style wines, etc.).

## Tasting vocabulary
When describing wines, use concrete, sensory language. Avoid abstract "notes of philosophy" prose.

Good: "Black cherry, a touch of leather, soft tannins."
Avoid: "An exquisite expression of terroir."

Standard descriptors by structural element:

- **Acidity**: crisp, bright, fresh, mouthwatering, racy.
- **Tannin**: soft, fine-grained, grippy, firm, drying.
- **Body**: light, medium, full, weighty.
- **Finish**: short, lingering, persistent.

## South African wine context
- Major wine regions: Stellenbosch, Franschhoek, Paarl, Constantia, Walker Bay, Elgin, Swartland, Hemel-en-Aarde.
- Signature varietals: Chenin Blanc (SA's flagship white), Pinotage (SA's signature red, a Pinot Noir × Cinsault crossing), Cabernet Sauvignon, Sauvignon Blanc, Chardonnay, Syrah/Shiraz.
- Major awards bodies and ratings: Platter's Wine Guide (5-star is the top), Tim Atkin Report, Veritas Awards, Decanter, Old Mutual Trophy Wine Show.
- A "Cape Vintners Classification" or "Premier Cape Wine" reference signals premium quality.

## Pricing context (SA market, mid-2025)
Prices are in **South African Rand (R)**. Typical retail price tiers:

- Entry: R80–R150 per bottle.
- Mid: R150–R350.
- Premium: R350–R700.
- Icon / cellar: R700+.

Quote prices the customer will actually pay. Don't editorialise on whether a price is "expensive" unless the customer raises it.

## Format conventions
- Vintages as four-digit years: "the 2021" not "the '21".
- Use "varietal" for the grape, "cultivar" only if the account does.
- Volume defaults to 750ml unless otherwise noted.
